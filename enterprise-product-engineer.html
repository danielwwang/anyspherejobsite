Product Engineer, Enterprise

Role

Product engineers create the software we ship to our users, in particular our editor. As a member of our core product team, you'll join us in inventing a new, better way to build software. In this role, you'll partner with the GTM team to build Cursor Enterprise, supporting the best engineering teams in the world.

This is a rare opportunity where a PLG motion is getting pushed from the top down - every CTO wants to move to a more AI-native world.

Our Enterprise Impact

  • Over 60% of Fortune 1,000 have engineers using Cursor
  • Over 1 billion lines of enterprise code generated per day
  • 50,000+ enterprises including NVIDIA, Uber, Adobe, OpenAI, Uber, Stripe, and Samsung
  • Fastest enterprise adoption in developer tools history with CTOs driving top-down AI transformation

You might be a fit if...

  • You have an entrepreneurial spirit and love creating outsized business impact.
  • You want to be at the frontier of AI transformation with the best companies in the world.
  • You're passionate about building great products that blend excellent engineering with a taste for models and design.
  • You have a propensity for creative ideas and have a knack for making powerful tools without compromising their ease-of-use.

About

We're Anysphere, the team behind Cursor.

Our mission is to automate coding. The first step in our journey is to build the best tool for professional programmers, using a combination of inventive research, design, and engineering.

We're a group of engineers and scientists who've built well-known products, created large OSS projects, started productive businesses, won olympiad medals, and published significant research.

Our organization is very flat, and our team is small and talent dense. We particularly like people who are truthseeking, passionate, and creative. We enjoy spirited debate, crazy ideas, and shipping code.

We're in-person in a cozy office in North Beach, San Francisco, replete with a well-stocked library. Our investors include Andreessen Horowitz, OpenAI, Jeff Dean, John Schulman, and the founders of Stripe and Github.
